Content Strategy

I almost quit.

Published by Your Name on August 10, 2025

My brain felt all mixed up. Posting things online every day is hard!

Should I tell a story about my day? Or talk about the things I sell? It was exhausting.

Then, I heard about a secret content strategy from Russel Brunson that was inspired by an old TV show called Seinfeld.

That show was about… nothing! Just funny things that happened in people's everyday lives. But at the end of each story, they would smoothly connect it to something else.

"Daily Seinfeld content" is an entertainment-based content marketing strategy that uses personal stories to connect with readers while subtly promoting an offer.

You can do this too! You don’t have to choose between sharing your story and sharing your offer. You can do both!

Here’s the super simple 1-2-3 plan:

  • Hook: Start with something that makes people stop scrolling.
  • Story: Tell a little story about your day. Anything! What you learned, what made you laugh, what you struggled with. People love real stories.
  • Offer: Gently connect your story to what you do. It can be a small nudge like, "If you liked this, you'll love my newsletter!" or something bigger.

This plan gives you so much freedom! No more headaches.

See? I just used this 1-2-3 plan in this post.
